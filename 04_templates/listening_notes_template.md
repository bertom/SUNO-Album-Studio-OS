# Listening Notes

Use for `/listen` — one section per run. Append new runs; don't delete failed ones.

---

## Run

Run ID, date, take letters if multiple (e.g. Run 004 — take A/B).

---

## Prompt Version

Link: `suno/suno_prompt_vN.md`

---

## Audio File

Filename in `audio/` (e.g. `run004_take_a.wav`).

---

## First Feeling

Gut reaction in user's words or close paraphrase. Capture within minutes of listening.

---

## What Worked

Specific elements: groove, vocal moment, chorus, bass, atmosphere, lyric delivery.

---

## What Failed

Specific elements — not "didn't like it."

---

## Vocal

Lead delivery, harmonies, group parts, language, sincerity, polish level.

---

## Groove

Rhythm body, pulse, movement, reggae sway, percussion, static vs alive.

---

## Lyrics

Clarity, singability, emotional landing, SUNO misreads, wrong words.

---

## Arrangement

Intro, build, chorus size, bridge, outro, hmm-hmm artifacts, repetition issues.

---

## Emotional Truth

Does it feel discovered or manufactured? Truth recognized in a body?

---

## Album Fit

Role on album, contrast with adjacent tracks, energy tier correct?

---

## SUNO Album Studio Fit

DNA check — which markers present or missing?

---

## SUNO Artifacts

Model quirks: nonsense words, wrong language, comedic edges, generic intro, vocal glitch.

---

## Decision

**keep exploring** / **shortlist** / **retry** / **select final** / **abandon direction**

---

## Next Prompt Move

One concrete change for next run. Map user feedback via `feedback_dictionary.md`.

---

## Learning Candidate

If reusable: tag for `/extract learning` — worked / failed / pattern.

---

## Example Entry

### Run 002 — 2026-06-28

**Prompt version:** suno_prompt_v1.md  
**Audio:** run002_take_a.wav

**First feeling:** Warm but too sleepy — missing body.

**What worked:** Vocal sincerity, Dutch pronunciation, gentle opening image.

**What failed:** No rhythmic invitation; floats after chorus one.

**Decision:** retry

**Next prompt move:** Add hand claps, soft reggae offbeat guitar, "embodied groove" — see "too mellow" in feedback dictionary.

**Learning candidate:** Acoustic-only Styles without pulse → passive on children's calm tracks too.
