# Lyric Principles

Sensible defaults — edit to match your voice.

## Core rules

1. **Singable first** — if you can't say it in one breath, shorten it.
2. **Life beats concept** — specific images beat abstract slogans.
3. **One idea per section** — verse explores; chorus lands.
4. **Mantra when earned** — repetition works when the line is true, not when you're stuck.
5. **Call-and-response in parentheses** — SUNO reads `(yeah)` and `(oh)` as backing vocals; use sparingly for lift.

## Tone

Defaults below — **edit to match your voice** after onboarding.

- Direct, embodied — not self-help, not preaching (unless that *is* your voice).
- Questions welcome; answers optional.
- Avoid moralizing and forced profundity.

## Structure defaults

| Section | Job |
|---------|-----|
| Verse | Scene, detail, movement |
| Pre-chorus | Tension or turn (optional) |
| Chorus | The line you'd tattoo — or wouldn't, but you'd hum |
| Bridge | New angle or release |
| Outro | Land or fade — don't over-explain |

## SUNO hygiene

- No artist names in lyrics fields.
- Sensitive words: see `03_global_learnings/suno_learnings.md` (e.g. avoid `skank` in styles — use "offbeat guitar chop" in Styles if needed; check lyrics separately).
